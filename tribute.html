Les Paul

The innovative guitarist and inventor who revolutionized the music industry

Les Paul performing live with his signature Gibson "Les Paul model" guitar

Notable moments in the life of Les Paul:

  • 1915 Born in Waukesha, Wisconsin
  • 1923 Begins playing guitar and invents a harmonica holder for hands free performance
  • 1928 At age 13, Les Paul was already performing semi-professionally, playing country music on guitar, banjo, and harmonica
  • 1932 At age 17, he dropped out of high school and teams up with Sunny Joe Wolverton's radio band in St. Louis, MO
  • 1934 Moves to Chicago wth Wolverton, playing country music on radio by day and jazz clubs at night
  • 1936 First 2 records released under the name "Rhubarb Red"
  • 1937 Forms a trio with Jim Atkins and Ernie "Darius" Newton
  • 1938 The trio leaves Chicago and moves to New York, landing a feature spot on Fred Waring's radio show
  • 1941 Approaches Gibson with a solid body electric guitar concept
  • 1943 Drafted into the U.S. Army, serving on the Armed forces radio networl
  • 1944 Plays with Nat King Cole for the Jazz at the Philharmonic Concert in Los Angeles
  • 1945 Records a #1 hit with Bing Crosby entitled "It's Been a Long, Long Time"
  • 1946 His trio tours as an opening act for The Andrews Sisters
  • 1948 Introduces his studio recording techniques called the "New Sound" which attracted newfound respect from the music industry
  • 1952 Gibson begins producing the "Les Paul model" solid body guitar to compete with Fender's "Telecaster" design
  • 1954 "The Les Paul Show" airs on NBC radio featuring the Les Paul Trio and Mary Ford
  • 1965 Goes into semi-retirement recording periodically afterwards
  • 2006 Wins 2 Grammy awards for the album "Les Paul & Friends: American Played World Made"
  • 2009 Dies from complications with pneumonia

Every setback might be the very thing that makes you carry on and fight all the harder and become much better.
-Les Paul
